SCORE Adaptive Bridges from Delta Software Technology GmbH is the quickest and easiest way of generating ready-to-run JSR 168 Java portlets to connect existing legacy applications and databases into an enterprise portal. When developing this product Delta decided to standardize on the eXo platform for development and testing. The decision was driven by comprehensive support for the JSR 168 standard, the simplicity of the implementation, the runtime performance and the complete ready-to-use installation.

The Delta developers downloaded eXo from SourceForge, unpacked the installation files and it ran first time. Unfortunately, the same cannot be said of a number of the leading commercial portal products that were tested. Delta has also decided to include the eXo platform with the upcoming trialware version of SCORE Adaptive Bridges. eXo platform provides a JSR 168 environment that works "out of the box", and this is what Delta's customers are looking for.

Music Interactive International AS is developing Performing Arts Management (PAM) services that provide efficient interaction between all participants in the live music industry. A central piece of the company's infrastructure is the eXo platform. The PAM services exploit web centric communication technologies, with mobile access, for simplified processes and interactions between all the participants involved in establishing a music performance. Leveraging such complex interactive services mandates easy to use and standards based technology, such as the portlet standard and eXo platform.

The PAM services address the whole value chain of the live music industry. The artist, as the prime creator of value, builds his craft, reputation and audience through performing. The PAM services link the artists with the professional intermediaries of the trade, and with their audiences. The services are unique in offering solutions addressing all relevant business processes.
